Our History

Our forefathers needed a sacred space to practice the rituals and customs of their country of birth and by 1886 they acquired land to build a temple. Today our Temple remains a monument to their commitment and sacrifices to propagate their culture. Over a period of over 100 years, many structural changes were made to the original Wood and Iron Temple, culminating in the current structure which was completed in 1994. New Murthis, Navagraharra, a Nandi and a Palpeedam were commissioned to be made in India. Thereafter the Khumba Abishegnam was observed. This followed by an Ananarta in 2006. As we celebrate another Anavarta, we pay tribute to all those who have contributed so much to ensure that we have a place of worship. The protection of Hindu Dharma, rests with Temples, Gurus and the Devotees that attend regular prayers, services and satsangs. At the Shree Kamatcheeammen Temple the flame of our great religion continues to burn bright. We are deeply privileged to do the vital work to maintain and protect our Sanatana Dharma for future generation.

May our Divine Mother shower her richest blessing upon you
